Key Findings
1
Technology drives highest absolute profit. Furniture is margin-negative primarily due to Tables (−$17,725) and Bookcases (−$3,473) sub-categories dragging the entire category down.
2
West region is the strongest performer in both revenue ($725K) and profit ($108K). Central region is the weakest with only 7.9% profit margin despite $501K in revenue.
3
Discounts above 20% are strongly correlated with losses (r ≈ −0.22 on profit; −0.55 on margin). Approximately 18.7% of all orders are unprofitable — a critical operational risk.
4
Corporate segment shows the best revenue-per-customer ratio. Home Office has the highest margin efficiency, making it the premium segment to nurture.
5
RFM analysis reveals ~25% of customers are Champions or Loyal. At-risk and lost customers represent significant revenue recovery opportunity requiring targeted retention campaigns.
Strategic Recommendations
→
Cap discounts at 20% across all categories. Implement a discount approval workflow for any order above this threshold to prevent margin erosion.
→
Divest or reprice loss-making Furniture sub-categories. Tables and Bookcases should either be repriced with a minimum 15% margin target or removed from the catalogue.
→
Focus marketing investment on West & East regions where ROI is proven. Conduct a cost-structure audit in the Central region before increasing spend.
→
Design loyalty programmes targeting At-Risk customers. Use RFM segmentation to send personalised win-back campaigns with controlled discount offers (<20%).
→
Promote Same Day / First Class shipping for high-margin SKUs (Copiers, Phones, Accessories) — faster delivery correlates with higher customer satisfaction and repeat purchase rates.
